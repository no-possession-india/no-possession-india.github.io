One Family's Unending Wait

We Paid for a Home
in 2012.
We Are Still Waiting.

My father bought a flat in Gujarat over a decade ago. The builder took the money. A partner died. A court case began. And we — the buyers — have been handed a new date every few months for ten years, while our family's health, education, and dignity have slowly collapsed.

Written by the son of the buyer  ·  Gujarat, India  ·  2026

"Every time I call, he says 'I will give you a date.'
That date is the only thing we have received in 12 years."

— Son of the buyer, speaking directly
A Timeline of Broken Promises
Flat Purchased in Good Faith

My father buys a flat from a builder with multiple partners. Payment made in full. RERA does not yet exist in Gujarat — buyers have no formal protection. A dream of a stable home begins.

First Delays Begin

Possession is due. The builder begins giving dates — "come next month," "come in 3 months." The family waits patiently, trusting the process.

A Partner Dies — Everything Stops

One of the 6–7 partners in the builder firm passes away. Instead of resolving ownership and honouring commitments to buyers, the surviving partners go to court against each other. The buyers are collateral damage.

Health Crisis Begins at Home

My mother develops chronic migraines. My father begins drinking daily. The stress of homelessness-in-waiting, combined with financial strain and helplessness, breaks the family's health. My sisters' education suffers. I miss college opportunities despite good marks.

Legal Authorities Contacted — No Action

We reach out to legal aid and consumer authorities. They ask for the builder's email ID. When we ask the builder, he simply never sends it. The system requires paperwork the wrongdoer can refuse to provide.

Builder Refuses Even a Refund

"I will never give you interest," he says on the phone. No refund. No possession. Just another date. The family has now lost a decade of potential housing equity, savings, and mental health.

Court Case Still Pending — No End in Sight

The partner dispute drags on in court. We cannot afford a lawyer. We fear police extortion. We fear endless litigation. We are publishing this story because we have no other option.

The Barriers We Face

"Go to the police" — but the police demand bribes. We are the victims. Why should we pay to be heard?

Barrier: Police Corruption

"Hire a lawyer" — but lawyers cost money we don't have, for a case we don't know will end in our lifetime.

Barrier: Cost of Justice

"File a RERA complaint" — but RERA didn't exist when we bought. Old cases have no clear protection.

Barrier: No Retroactive RERA

"Get the builder's email" — he simply doesn't send it. Authorities need it to proceed. The wrongdoer controls the evidence.

Barrier: Procedural Deadlock

The builder's family moved to Mumbai. No known address. No accountability. No consequences — so far.

Barrier: Fugitive Impunity

Court cases in India take decades. For a family without resources, filing a case means years of cost with uncertain outcome.

Barrier: Judicial Backlog
What We Are Asking For
  • Immediate Possession or Full Refund with Interest
    The builder took money in 2012. Either deliver the flat or return every rupee with fair interest. This is not generosity — it is the law.
  • Gujarat Government: Create a Pre-RERA Dispute Cell
    Thousands of families bought flats before RERA existed. They deserve a dedicated, free dispute resolution mechanism — not silence.
  • Locate the Builder — His Family Has Moved to Mumbai
    The developer relocated without informing buyers or courts. Authorities must treat this as evidence of intent to evade — not a clerical matter.
  • Free Legal Aid for Housing Fraud Victims
    Victims of builder fraud should not need ₹50,000+ to hire a lawyer just to file a claim. State-sponsored legal aid for housing cases must be accessible and real.
  • Anti-Corruption Mechanism for Housing Complaints
    If police extort victims, there must be a safe, anonymous channel to report both the original fraud and the bribe demand — without fear.
